The Problem We're Solving

New agents have conversations every day — at open houses, over coffee, through referrals. But the value of those conversations vanishes almost immediately.

Conversations happen, information gets lost
Agents talk to people all day but don't capture what they learn. The details from a conversation on Tuesday are gone by Thursday.
Nothing goes into a retrievable format
Even when an agent remembers something, it's not written down anywhere searchable. It lives in their head, in scattered notes, in text threads they'll never find again.
No visibility into their own activity
Agents don't realize how many meaningful conversations they're having — or how many they're missing. There's no picture of their relationship-building work.
Follow-up breaks down
This is where deals die. Follow-up fails for three reasons:
The right information wasn't captured in the first place
When they come back to a contact later, there's not enough context to know what to do next
They're simply not reminded at the right time
Plus: coaching philosophy built into the process
The application doesn't just store information — it reinforces a coaching framework that guides agents through each relationship. The six-step progression is built into every contact, so good habits form naturally as they work.
Asked about plans → Understand the plan → Identified next step → Offered to help → Accepted help → Confirmed next step

Why Hodo

This application removes weight from a broker's day. It doesn't add more to do — it takes things off your plate. You talk, Frank listens, things get organized, nothing falls through the cracks. The stress of holding everything in your head dissolves.

The technology becomes invisible and you're just doing your work, naturally. No forms. No data entry. No feature overload. Just enough support that you can focus on what actually matters — the people in front of you.

The Philosophy Beneath
"We're not making anything. We're not creating. We're uncovering what's already there."

People already have plans. They're already moving — buying, selling, exploring, waiting, accelerating. This flow is a river. Hodo doesn't build the river or control its direction. It reveals it. It helps the broker see and engage with what's already happening in people's lives.

This is not a tool that pushes people through a funnel. It's a calm presence.

Target — New Agents

Our first market is new real estate agents who:

Feel overwhelmed and don't have a system
Want to build relationships, not cold-call
Need structure but don't want rigidity
Are looking for a mentor/partner, not a tool

From our interviews: they're relieved to find this. That's the emotion we're designing for.

Design Decisions Made
Contact detail layout
The Brief — narrative profile, not a field list. "Tell me about Marco."
Framework visualization
Progress Ring — quiet 5/6 ring in the plan card corner
Chat style
